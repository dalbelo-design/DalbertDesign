Mission
Better policy comes from better policy design.
Lived experience can tell you what’s wrong. Living expertise can help you build it right.
Most policy is designed without the people closest to the problem in the room. The result is investment that doesn’t reach who it’s meant to — programs that miss, services that strain, dollars that get spent without the outcomes anyone hoped for.

LELI changes the design table. We prepare community members with lived experience to co-author the work — analyzing public input, surfacing structural barriers, translating community knowledge into the language of decision-making, and bringing it directly into the rooms where policy gets shaped.

The outcome is policy that works the first time. Investment that produces. Systems that actually serve.

When people with lived experience help design the policy, the policy levels up. The dollars do more. The systems do better.

Lived Experience
Tells you what is wrong
The direct knowledge of what it is like to navigate systems not built for you. It surfaces the gaps that data does not capture and that people inside the system often cannot see.
Living Expertise
Helps you build it right
What develops when someone refines their lived experience into the capacity to actively help design better systems. It builds on lived experience — it never replaces it.
The Fellowship
What a Fellow builds.
Fellows don’t arrive needing to be filled. They arrive carrying expertise systems have refused to recognize.

The Fellowship is where lived expertise gets named, sharpened, and brought into the design of public investment. Fellows develop the skills, relationships, and tools that stay in the community — capacity owned by the people who built it.

01
A leadership identity rooted in self-validation.
Fellows develop a strong sense of who they are and what they know — so they can show up in policymaking spaces with the confidence the work requires.
02
Translating experience into policy-grade insight.
Fellows analyze public comment and community input, surface patterns and structural barriers, and produce findings that decision-makers can actually use.
03
Co-authoring the policies that shape their lives.
Fellows present at city councils, county hearings, and state meetings — moving from public comment to co-design, contributing directly to how investment gets allocated.
04
Building durable civic capacity.
The work doesn’t end with a grant cycle. Fellows leave with skills, relationships, and tools that stay in the community — capacity owned by the people who built it.
Who Fellows become
Fellows are not returning to their previous circumstances at the end of the program. They are building toward a growing ecosystem of paid, embedded roles inside county and state systems — roles that LELI is actively helping to create. The goal is for LELI Fellows to become the people decision-makers call when they need someone who can operate in high-stakes institutional environments with both lived insight and analytical skill.
