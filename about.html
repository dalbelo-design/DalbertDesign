About the partnership

Designed from the inside out.

We are a partnership between a lived experience–led design firm and a decade-old human-centered design consultancy — offering what most organizations can't: methodology grounded in genuine community knowledge.

D
Organization one
Dalbert Design
Lived experience, institutionalized
Housing Systems Lived Experience Contra Costa County

Dalbert Design is a lived experience–led organization founded by Loren Dalbert and based in Contra Costa County. Our work integrates the direct knowledge of navigating homelessness and housing systems with the analytical capacity to translate that knowledge into institutional action.

We co-author the systems that govern people's lives alongside the departments that administer them — not as outside critics, but as contributors to systems that work better for everyone inside them. Our county partnerships currently span seven departments including Contra Costa Health, Employment and Human Services, Behavioral Health, and the Housing Authority of Contra Costa County.

Loren came to this work through direct experience. A VA housing case manager encouraged him to apply for a lived experience consultant role on a county project connecting homelessness services to workforce development. He later co-led a Workforce Accelerator Fund initiative and helped catalyze the CoCo Go BIG guaranteed income pilot and $4.25 million in county funding.

Organization two
CivicMakers
Human-centered design for public good
Service Design Strategic Planning Bay Area & Beyond

CivicMakers is a strategic consultancy and community of practitioners that believes in the power of inclusive, collaborative design to shape a better world. Founded over a decade ago in San Francisco, we partner with governments, nonprofits, and mission-driven organizations to unearth human insights and co-create solutions to public challenges.

We specialize in four core service areas: Service Design & Evaluation; Strategic Planning; Training & Capacity Building; and Community & Stakeholder Engagement. Our team of pragmatic optimists brings multidisciplinary experience from across the public, private, and social sectors.

We've worked with agencies across California — from the CA Health and Human Services Agency to city departments in San José, San Rafael, Fremont, and San Francisco. Our approach: we design with communities, not for them.

What guides us

Principles we actually design by.

These aren't aspirations — they're methodological commitments that shape every engagement, every room we walk into, and every deliverable we produce.

Design with, not for

Community is not a monolith. We take time to understand who is impacted and build engagement strategies relevant to local needs.

Dignity before data

Lived experience is not an input to be extracted. We compensate advisors and build structures that make contribution meaningful.

Trust as infrastructure

We build relationships before we build plans — understanding how community members want to be engaged, then maintaining those bonds long after.

Capacity over dependency

We share our tools with every partner. The goal is for your team to lead the work long after we step back.

Pragmatic optimism

We identify real leverage points and build durable change there — we don't oversell transformation.

Actionable feedback loops

We create loops that bring people along from discovery through development — so those engaged see themselves in the end product.

Current initiative

The Lived Experience Leadership Institute

Our signature program — where our partnership methodology becomes a training program for the next generation of civic leaders.

LELI — Cohort 1 launching August 2026

The Lived Experience Leadership Institute trains fellows ages 18–26 from five unincorporated Contra Costa County communities — Rodeo, Crockett, Pacheco, Vine Hill, and Clyde — in civic intelligence research, AI-directed analysis, and policy engagement. Funded through a Marathon and Phillips 66 Community Benefits Agreement.
